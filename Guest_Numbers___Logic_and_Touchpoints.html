1  —  Old approach vs new approach
Retiring — old approach
Guest counts derived from products
  • Day guests = Wedding Breakfast menu quantity
  • Evening guests = Evening Food quantity minus Day guests
  • Children = Children's Menu quantity
Breaks when food quantities exceed actual headcount — e.g. 100 guests plus 20 extra cheese boards = 120 on the line, not 120 guests.
New — agreed approach
Guest Tab is the source of truth
Guest Tab holds definitive headcount. Products record quantities of food and drink items only — they are no longer the source of headcount data. The two are intentionally decoupled.

3  —  Guest Tab — what it holds
Source of truth
Guest Tab stores per-guest records
  • Guest name and email
  • Guest type: Day / Evening / Day & Evening
  • Age band: Adult, Teen, Child, Baby
  • Dietary requirements and allergens
  • Flag for accommodation required
Open question
Evening guest count — total or additional?
If 80 day guests stay for the evening and 40 additional evening guests arrive, is the Evening guest count 120 (total evening attendance) or 40 (additional only)? This affects the Chef Sheet, reporting, and capacity checks.
